Hi, my name is Nathan. This is a website I'm working on as a bit of a showcase of physics-related things I find interesting. Eventually I'll write something here about why I love physics so much, but, for now, I hope that my love of physics comes across in the stuff on this website.


A little about me.


I've recently graduated from Yale with a B.S. in mathematics and physics (I like math too). I apparently did well enough in undergrad to get the Yale Science and Engineering Association's senior of distinction and Howard L. Schultz awards, both for physics. My thesis was on vortex excitations in the Gross-Pitaevskii model. While physics is my first love, I also dedicate some time to artistic pursuits, including writing (fiction, nonfiction, poetry) and drawing. In undergrad, for example, the talented Emily Cai and I headed visual arts for The Cortex Collective, an arts and sciences publication. One piece I'm proud of is this one about a sentient colony of naked mole rats. It's fun to explore abstract ideas (such as emergence, group think, etc.) in wacky settings.
