This section's a bit of a work in progress. I'm thinking I'll use it to upload some little tid-bits (mostly physical derivations) I find interesting. Some ideas include:


  • Parametric excitation and the physics of swings. I think the physics of pumping on a swing (or of parametric excitation/instability more generally) is really interesting.

  • Spinors. Spinors are kind of wild. The quantization of angular momentum makes perfect sense when described in terms of spherical harmonics, and the somewhat confusing notion of "intrinsic angular momentum" becomes a lot clearer once the Wigner-Eckart theorem has been introduced. Neither of these, however, make spinors any less confusing—no spherical harmonic could ever lead to angular momentum $\hbar/2$, and what on earth is a rank-$1/2$ tensor? I came across an argument in André Mercier's textbook Analyatical and Canonical Formalism in Physics showing rank-$1/2$ tensors are, in fact, a natural part of any space with "isotropic vectors" (non-zero vectors with zero norm). Since Minkowski space has isotropic vectors (anything on the light cone), spinors must exist. I think it'd be a fun exercise to work through this argument (which is originally due to Marcel Riesz). As far as I can tell, this is not common knowledge in the physics world.
